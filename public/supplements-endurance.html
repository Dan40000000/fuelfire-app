Endurance & Stamina Supplements

Evidence-Based Guide for Peak Performance & Sustained Energy

Medical Disclaimer

This information is for educational purposes only and is not intended as medical advice. Always consult with a healthcare professional before starting any supplement regimen, especially if you have medical conditions or take medications.

Essential: Hydration & Electrolytes

Before any supplement strategy, ensure proper hydration with electrolytes (sodium, potassium, magnesium). Dehydration significantly impairs endurance performance regardless of supplementation.

Beta-Alanine
★★★★★ 5/5
PRE DURING
High-Intensity Cardio Strength Endurance HIIT
Optimal Dosage & Timing

Dosage: 3-5g daily, divided into 2-3 doses
Timing: 30-60 minutes pre-workout or with meals
Duration: 4-6 weeks to reach muscle saturation

Loading Protocol

Week 1-2: 2-3g daily
Week 3+: 3-5g daily for maintenance
Take with meals to reduce tingling sensation

Key Benefits
  • Reduces muscle acidosis and fatigue
  • Improves performance in 1-4 minute high-intensity efforts
  • Increases muscle carnosine levels
  • Delays onset of muscular fatigue
  • Enhances repeated sprint performance
Side Effects & Cautions

Common: Harmless tingling sensation (paresthesia)
Avoid if: Sensitive to tingling sensations
Note: Effects diminish if supplementation stops

Quality Indicators

Look for: CarnoSyn® branded beta-alanine, third-party tested, pharmaceutical grade, no artificial fillers

Creatine Monohydrate
★★★★★ 5/5
POST
Strength Endurance Power Endurance Recovery
Optimal Dosage & Timing

Dosage: 3-5g daily
Timing: Post-workout with carbs or anytime
Consistency: Daily supplementation required

Loading Options

Fast Loading: 20g daily for 5 days, then 3-5g maintenance
Slow Loading: 3-5g daily for 3-4 weeks
Both methods achieve same saturation

Key Benefits
  • Improves repeated high-intensity efforts
  • Increases muscle power and strength
  • Faster recovery between sets
  • Enhanced muscle volume and hydration
  • Supports ATP regeneration
Side Effects & Cautions

Common: Initial water weight gain (1-3 lbs)
Rare: Digestive upset with large doses
Hydration: Increase water intake

Quality Indicators

Look for: Creapure® brand, micronized powder, NSF certified, no added sugars or flavors

Citrulline Malate
★★★★☆ 4/5
PRE
Cardio Endurance Strength Endurance Recovery
Optimal Dosage & Timing

Dosage: 6-8g citrulline malate (2:1 ratio)
Timing: 30-45 minutes pre-workout
Empty stomach: For optimal absorption

Key Benefits
  • Increases nitric oxide production
  • Improves blood flow and oxygen delivery
  • Reduces muscle soreness
  • Enhances exercise capacity
  • Faster recovery between sets
Side Effects & Cautions

Rare: Mild digestive upset with high doses
Interaction: May enhance effects of blood pressure medications
Start low: Begin with 4-6g to assess tolerance

Quality Indicators

Look for: 2:1 citrulline to malate ratio, pharmaceutical grade, third-party tested, no artificial additives

Beetroot Extract (Nitrates)
★★★★☆ 4/5
PRE
Aerobic Endurance Cycling Running
Optimal Dosage & Timing

Dosage: 300-600mg nitrates (equivalent to 500ml beetroot juice)
Timing: 2-3 hours pre-exercise for peak effect
Duration: Effects last 6-8 hours

Key Benefits
  • Improves oxygen efficiency
  • Reduces oxygen cost of exercise
  • Enhances time to exhaustion
  • Improves performance in 4-30 minute efforts
  • Natural vasodilation
Side Effects & Cautions

Harmless: Pink/red urine (beeturia)
Avoid if: History of kidney stones
Note: Avoid mouthwash 6 hours before use (reduces effectiveness)

Quality Indicators

Look for: Standardized nitrate content, organic beetroot, concentrated extract, no added sugars

Iron (If Deficient)
★★★★★ 5/5
POST
Oxygen Transport All Endurance Types Recovery
Optimal Dosage & Timing

Test first: Blood test to confirm deficiency
Dosage: 18-65mg elemental iron (as prescribed)
Timing: Between meals or with vitamin C

Key Benefits (If Deficient)
  • Improves oxygen-carrying capacity
  • Reduces fatigue and breathlessness
  • Enhances aerobic performance
  • Supports hemoglobin production
  • Critical for endurance athletes
Important Cautions

ONLY if deficient: Excess iron is harmful
Test required: Ferritin, hemoglobin, iron studies
Side effects: Constipation, nausea, stomach upset
Monitor: Regular blood tests during supplementation

Quality Indicators

Look for: Chelated forms (bisglycinate), gentle on stomach, includes vitamin C, avoid with calcium/coffee

B-Complex Vitamins
★★★★☆ 4/5
PRE POST
Energy Metabolism All Endurance Types Recovery
Optimal Dosage & Timing

Dosage: High-potency B-complex (50-100mg B vitamins)
Timing: Morning with breakfast or pre-workout
Key vitamins: B1, B2, B3, B6, B12, folate

Key Benefits
  • Essential for energy metabolism
  • Supports nervous system function
  • Reduces exercise-induced stress
  • Aids in recovery and repair
  • Prevents deficiency in high-training athletes
Side Effects & Cautions

Common: Bright yellow urine (harmless)
Rare: Nausea if taken on empty stomach
Note: Water-soluble, excess is excreted

Quality Indicators

Look for: Active forms (methylcobalamin B12, P5P B6), complete spectrum, third-party tested

Caffeine + L-Theanine
★★★★★ 5/5
PRE
Mental Focus Power Endurance Fat Burning
Optimal Dosage & Timing

Dosage: 100-200mg caffeine + 200-400mg L-theanine
Ratio: 2:1 theanine to caffeine
Timing: 30-45 minutes pre-workout

Key Benefits
  • Enhanced focus and alertness
  • Improved fat oxidation
  • Reduced perceived exertion
  • Smooth energy without jitters
  • Increased time to exhaustion
Side Effects & Cautions

Common: Jitters, anxiety without L-theanine
Timing: Avoid within 6 hours of sleep
Tolerance: Cycle to maintain effectiveness

Quality Indicators

Look for: Natural caffeine sources, Suntheanine® L-theanine, proper 2:1 ratio, no artificial stimulants

Branched Chain Amino Acids (BCAAs)
★★☆☆☆ 2/5
DURING POST
Ultra-Endurance Fasted Training Recovery
Optimal Dosage & Timing

Dosage: 10-15g in 2:1:1 ratio (leucine:isoleucine:valine)
Timing: During long sessions or post-workout
Best for: Fasted training or sessions >90 minutes

Key Benefits
  • May reduce muscle protein breakdown
  • Potential fatigue reduction in long sessions
  • Muscle recovery support
  • Useful during fasted cardio
  • Maintains muscle mass during cutting
Important Notes

Limited evidence: Benefits minimal if eating adequate protein
Better alternatives: Complete protein sources
Cost-effective: Whole foods often superior

Quality Indicators

Look for: 2:1:1 ratio, instantized powder, no artificial fillers, third-party tested

Key Takeaways for Endurance Supplementation

Priority Order: 1) Proper nutrition & hydration 2) Creatine & Beta-alanine 3) Caffeine + L-theanine 4) Citrulline malate 5) Address deficiencies (iron, B-vitamins)

Remember: Supplements enhance a solid training and nutrition foundation - they don't replace it

Testing: Always test supplements during training, never on competition day
